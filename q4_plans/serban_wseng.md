Insights & Key Learnings
High throughput is meaningless without a stable strategy.
While we successfully increased our raw output (SUs), much of that effort was neutralized by churn and rework.
The constantly changing product direction meant that work was often discarded or significantly altered.
Many of our teams have been primarily deleting or significantly rebuilding features that they shipped one or two quarters ago.
Our primary bottleneck is not engineering execution, but the absence of a clear and stable product vision.
Personnel instability has become our most significant operational risk.
The unplanned attrition and extended leaves this quarter exposed significant vulnerabilities.
Key knowledge is concentrated in a few individuals, and we lack the redundancy to handle unexpected departures.
This created single points of failure (e.g., Rezgar, Denys) that directly impacted our ability to execute on strategic initiatives.
When faced with numerous fires, we lacked the discipline to focus on operational improvements.
Continuous improvement efforts were almost entirely sacrificed in favor of reactive firefighting this quarter.
The constant churn and volatility consumed all available bandwidth, preventing us from focusing on our own operational enhancements.
The few improvements that were implemented (e.g., team rebalancing, BrainLift workflow adjustments) yielded positive results.
Still, we were unable to dedicate the focused time needed to address the backlog of key operational goals.

Current Quarter Plan (Q4 2025)
Strategic Priorities
Stabilize product development with a dual-stream strategy.
To counteract the volatility that defined Q3, we will structure all engineering work into two parallel streams. This will insulate a significant portion of the team from short-term product churn and allow for stable, long-term progress on foundational assets.
Product Feature Stream: Focused on new user-facing capabilities, this stream will be governed by a mandatory written requirements process. No major feature work will begin without being written down: requirements, problem statement, and background context.
Strategic Initiatives Stream: An engineering-led program with a stable, pre-approved roadmap focused on modernizing platforms and reducing operational drag. Q4 initiatives include: Incept, AI-First Admissions, replace/rebuild the SIS, and the CoachBot Sunset.

Resolve the Product Leadership Gap.
Filling this gap is our single most important prerequisite for long-term success. This will be a primary focus in Q4.
We will diversify our hiring pipelines to find the right combination of strategic and tactical leadership:
SVP / Chief Product Officer ($200/hr) - part of the LwAI Budget.
VP / Lead Product Owner ($100/hr)
Product Owners ($50/hr)

Stretch goal: revamp the UI/UX design function.
We will replace the current designer with a $100/h role that better fits our iterative, AI-first workflow.
Design will be split into two main work activities:
Proactive guardrails: Help Product identify only the critical UX constraints early.
Reactive reviews: Evaluate shipped UIs and give feedback for next iterations.

Operational Goals
Re-commit to continuous operational improvement.
In Q3, proactive improvements were sacrificed for reactive firefighting. In Q4, this becomes a formal priority with time dedicated to refining our internal workflows, tools, and processes.
Success criteria: Clear the backlog of key operational goals from prior quarters:
Publish the reusable LLM-based chat pattern.
Finalize the full migration of all projects from Jira to GitHub Issues.
Finalize the transition to AI-led code reviews for all PRs.
Mitigate single points of failure in key engineering roles.
The instability in Q3 highlighted significant risks from knowledge concentration.
To address this, we will: Hire a Lead Engineer ($100/hr) with a focus on building platforms to reduce dependency on key individuals.
Financial Performance
Maintain stable delivery while focusing on the foundations of our products. Target ~11,000 SUs (same target as Q3).
Our Q4 delivery target is ~11,000 Story Units, matching our Q3 goals.

This investment in our foundation is critical for long-term velocity and stability.
